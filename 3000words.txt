Main Faqs

What is OUCRU Ho Chi Minh?
OUCRU Ho Chi Minh stands for the Oxford University Clinical Research Unit in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. It's a research unit dedicated to studying infectious diseases

Who runs OUCRU Ho Chi Minh?
OUCRU Ho Chi Minh is a collaboration between Oxford University and the Hospital for Tropical Diseases in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.

Where is OUCRU Ho Chi Minh located?
OUCRU Ho Chi Minh is located in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam.

What are the focus areas of OUCRU Ho Chi Minh?
OUCRU Ho Chi Minh focuses on clinical research related to infectious diseases, particularly those relevant to the tropical and developing world context. This includes diseases such as malaria, tuberculosis, dengue, influenza, and emerging infectious diseases.

What types of research activities does OUCRU Ho Chi Minh undertake?
OUCRU Ho Chi Minh conducts a wide range of research activities, including epidemiological studies, clinical trials, basic science research, and translational research.

Who does OUCRU Ho Chi Minh collaborate with?
OUCRU Ho Chi Minh collaborates with various national and international partners, including research institutions, universities, government agencies, and non-governmental organizations.

What is the composition of the staff at OUCRU Ho Chi Minh?
The unit comprises a multidisciplinary team of researchers, clinicians, scientists, and support staff from diverse backgrounds.

What facilities does OUCRU Ho Chi Minh have?
OUCRU Ho Chi Minh is equipped with state-of-the-art laboratories and clinical facilities to support its research activities.
